About Me

My Interest in this field began when I took my first Computer Science related class in High School. The course had a more broadened range of Computer Science knowledge, but one of the subjects of this course was Web Development. Ironically, I was not particularly interested in trying to learn all of the Markup language that went into HTML and CSS, but the creative side of me was fascinated with the endless possibilities of the projects that I could create.

From there I began my journey at Rollins College, where I took my first Computer Science introduction class. Not only did I fall in love with Software Development, I was finally starting to grasp some of the concepts that pertain to Computer Science such as logical thinking and Object- Oriented Programming.

Although Rollins College didn't offer too many Web Development-related courses, there were enough to lay a foundation for me. One of the skills that Rollins College has instilled in me is the ability to teach myself new concepts and ideas. That is how I have gotten myself up to this point. With the knowledge that they have provided me in combination with my self-teaching ability, I have been able to consider myself capable of creating a web product that, not only am I satisfied with, but will please the wishes of any client, as well.

I am exceptionally optimistic about the future that I have in Web Development, and even more ecstatic that I am able to express my creative ideas and the ideas of others into a product that is beneficial to its users.
